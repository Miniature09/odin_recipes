Jjajangmyeon

Description

Jjajangmyeon(Noodles in Black Bean Sauce) is a Korean noodle dish topped with black bean sauce made of Chunjang[korean black bean paste], diced pork and vegetables. Jjajangmyeon is a very delicious and satisfying dish that can be made ready in minutes.

Ingredients

*For 6 servings
  • Fresh jjajangmyeon noodles(1kg)
  • 1 large onions
  • 1 medium Zucchini
  • 2 large potatoes
  • 7 large bottom mushrooms
  • 1/4 small cabbage
  • Diced pork(600g)
  • Pork marinade sauce
  • 6 Tbsp Chunjang
  • 6 Tbsp cooking oil(e.g rice bran oil)
  • 2 Tbsp brown sugar
  • 4 Tbsp rice wine(mirin)
  • 1 cup Chicken stock
  • 1 cup Water
    • Slurry

    Toppings(optional):

  • Cucumber
  • Peas or sweet corn
  • Hard boiled egg

*1 Tbsp = 15ml, 1 cup = 250ml

Steps

  1. Rinse the pirk in cold water and pat it dry with kitchen paper. Add the pork marinade sauce onto the pork. Mix them well and leave it for 15 mins.
  2. Cut the onion, Zucchini and potato into small cubes. Thin slice the mushrooms, cut the cabbage into small to medium size pieces and julienne cut the cucumber.
  3. Add cooking oil to the cook and let it melt. Add Chunjang and stir it constantly on medium heat for 3-5 mins. Add the brown sugar and stir for 2-3 mins.
  4. Scoop out the black bean paste(without the oil) and set it aside. Leave the oil in the wok to use in the next step.
  5. Add the pork into the wok and stir until the pork is half cooked. Add the onion, Zucchini, and potatoes and stir for 3-5 mins. Add the mushrooms and cabbages and stir for 2-3 mins.
  6. Add the black bean paste into the wok and mix it in with the vegetables. Stir for 1-2 mins. Add the chicken stock, water and rice wine then simmer it for 5-7 mins on medium heat(cover the wok with a lid to make cooking process faster).
  7. Add the Slurry into the wok then stir it.(This will thicken the black sauce)
  8. Boil some water in a pot and add the noodles. Boil them until the noodles are cooked.
  9. Rinse the noodles in cold water and drain. Put them into a serving bowl/plate. Add the black bean sauce on top of the noodles. Add some toppings if you want.
  10. Mix the sauce and the noodles well with chopsticks and Dig in!

Storage: The black bean sauce can last for 2 to 3 days in the fridge if you have leftover sauce. Keep it in an air tight container.
